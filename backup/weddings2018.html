Danesfield House Hotel, Marlow-on-Thames

Elegant sophistication was called for in this unique stunning venue. Steeped in history dating back to 1750, Danesfield House Hotel has certainly got some stories to tell. The latest being the splendid wedding of Emily & Richard. The choice of purple and lilac complimented the traditional romantic theme in the Versailles Suite, with it’s high ceilings and sparkling Chandeliers. A 17ft Garland full of rich Hydrangea lined the top table and the tables were adorned with candelabras full of flowers and light.

"Thank you so much Bishop & Polden. The flowers were beyond anything I could ever have imagined! I had some ideas to start off with, and you really ran with it and made it 100 times better, I loved it! Thanks for making our special day so beautiful." Emily and Richard, 2018

Landmark Arts Centre, Twickenham

What a venue for a wedding. This magnificent french gothic style building originally constructed in 1880 as the church of St Alban the martyr, has in more recent years been used as an art centre and venue. The high stone ceilings and arches allow light to flood the room. It truly is stunning. The wedding of Rachel and Adam was also stunning. With a wild yellow flower theme to contrast the bridesmaids' navy dresses, large balls of gyp in high glasses and loads of fairy lights and candles made this an original and unique theme. We think the photos speak for themselves.

Great Missenden Abbey, Buckinghamshire

This amazing abbey was founded in 1133 and has seen royals throughout history staying here. The restorations in 1806 is largely responsible for the Neo-Gothis structure we see today. It is truly a splendid venue and the most amazing backdrop for our bride and groom Eileen and Mitch. They chose a romantic theme, with soft hues and delicate lighting. A hurricane vase with a large candle surrounded with flowers adorned each table and a little glitz was added for impact, with the rose gold shimmering table runners.The stair case was lined with lots of foliage and a sprinkling of flowers, and the chair backs decorated with roses and foliage. The bouquets were free flowing and sophisticated. An extremely romantic wedding in a very romantic place.

"We can’t get over how fantastic the flowers were! We received so many compliments from both guests and Abbey staff. You managed to match the colours in the bouquets perfectly with the dresses, and the table decorations were outstanding. We just wanted you to know how thrilled we both were with all you did for us." Eileen and Mitch, 2018

The Swan Hotel, The Cotswolds

Located on the enchanting banks of the River Coln, this former Cotswold coaching inn was the perfect place for a romantic rustic wedding. Lisa chose vintage tea cups, which we filled with flowers and put everywhere, including the beautiful imposing stone fireplace and sitting on candelabras on each table. With the use of muted hues and lots of foliage we gave the theme a timeless elegant style. Just stunning.
